FAQs

service animals

What is a service animal?

A service animal is an animal that is trianed to perform tasks for an individual with a disability. These tasks must be directly related to the individual's disability. Service animals are NOT pets.

What are the rights of a service animal?

A service animal can accompany their handler to any facility where members of the public are allowed EVEN if there is a "no pets" policy. However, this is contingent on the fact that the animal wears an identification symbol which is commonly recognized as an indicator of a service animal

What are the responsibilites of a handler?

Handlers must always be in control of their service animals. The animal must me house broken and vaccinated.

non service animals

What are non service animals?

Non service animals are all animals that do not meet the deffinition of service animal. Such animals include: emotional support animals, therapy animals, comfort animals and others.

What is an emotional support animal?

Emotional support animals are animals that provide comfort just by being with a person. They have not been trained to perform a specific task and are not limited to working with people with disabilities, ergo they do not qualify as service animals.

At UM, where is my emotional support animal permitted?

Emotional support animals are allowed in the owner's campus residence, but are not allowed in university areas with animal restrictions(Classrooms, Shalala Center, Libraries, University Center, Transportation, etc).
